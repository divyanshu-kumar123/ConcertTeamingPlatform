Use Case & Requirement Document
Private Employee Concert – Seating Group & Allocation Management Platform
 
1. Purpose of the Platform
The purpose of this platform is to enable employees attending a private organizational concert to self‑organize into seating teams and later ensure proper seating allocation so that colleagues can sit together comfortably.
The platform also supports administrative control to:
Review all teams and individual employees.
Assign seats intelligently and manually where required.
Finalize and export seating arrangements.
 
2. Scope of the Platform
2.1 In Scope
Employee registration using official employee codes.
Employee‑driven invitation-based team formation.
Visibility of team and invitation status.
Handling uneven team sizes and individual participants.
Centralized seat allocation by admin.
Intelligent and manual seat assignment.
Export of final seating data in Excel format.
2.2 Out of Scope
Ticket purchase and payments.
Event access control or entry passes.
Seat selection by employees.
Event-day operational logistics.
 
3. Pre‑Event Data Preparation
3.1 Participant List
A master Excel file will be provided containing all employees registered for the event.
The file will include: 
SAP ID (Employee Code)
Employee Name
Any additional identification fields if available
Only employees from this list are eligible to use the platform.
3.2 Seating Configuration
There will be 8 fixed seating groups: 
Group A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H
Each seating group will have a predefined number of seats.
Seating capacity cannot be exceeded under any condition.
 
4. User Roles
4.1 Employee
Any registered event participant.
Can form or join teams.
Can send and accept invitations.
Has no leadership or privilege over others.
4.2 Admin
Admin does not require registration through the employee flow.
Admin access is created separately.
Admin has full visibility and control over: 
All teams
Individual (ungrouped) employees
Seating assignment
Data export
Admin access remains available even when team formation is closed.
 
5. Employee Registration & Login (UPDATED)
5.1 Registration Information
During registration, each employee must provide: 
SAP ID (Employee Code)
Official or personal Email ID
Password (created by the employee)
5.2 Email Verification
Email verification is mandatory for successful registration.
After submitting registration details: 
A one‑time verification code (OTP) will be sent to the provided email address.
The employee must: 
Enter the OTP to verify ownership of the email ID.
Complete registration only after successful OTP verification.
5.3 Verification Rules
Registration is considered incomplete until email verification is successful.
Only verified users can: 
Log in to the platform
Search employees
Send or receive invitations
OTP is: 
Valid for a limited time
Required only during initial registration or email change (if allowed later)
5.4 Email Uniqueness
One email ID can be linked to only one SAP ID.
An SAP ID cannot be registered with multiple email IDs.
 
5.5 Login Rules (Unchanged with Clarification)
Employees log in using: 
SAP ID
Password
Login is allowed only after successful email verification.
 
5.6 Admin Exemption
Admin access: 
Does not require employee registration.
Does not require email OTP verification through the employee flow.
Admin credentials are created and managed separately.
 
 
6. Employee Search
6.1 Search Options
Employees can search other registered participants by: 
SAP ID
Employee Name
6.2 Search Assistance
Search results should appear dynamically as the user types.
Only registered participants should appear in suggestions.
Each result should clearly show name and SAP ID.
 
7. Invitation & Team Formation
7.1 Sending Invitations
Any employee can send invitations to one or more employees.
Invitations are for joining the same seating team.
7.2 Receiving Invitations
Employees can view received invitations after login.
Invitation details include: 
Sender name
Sender SAP ID
7.3 Accepting or Rejecting Invitations
Employees can accept or reject invitations.
Accepting an invitation merges both employees into one team.
7.4 Team Expansion Rules
Any team member can invite more employees.
There is no team leader.
Teams grow based purely on accepted invitations.
 
8. Team Definition & Outcomes
8.1 Team Structure
A team consists of employees connected through accepted invitations.
Teams may vary in size.
8.2 Uneven Groups
After team formation: 
Some teams may be small or large.
Some employees may remain individual, not part of any team.
Individual employees must still be assigned seats later.
 
9. Team Formation Closure
Team formation is open for a defined period.
After closure: 
No new invitations can be sent or accepted by employees.
Team structures become final for seating purposes.
Admin access is not affected by this closure.
 
10. Seat Allocation Overview
10.1 Allocation Timing
Seat allocation happens after team formation is completed.
However, admin may initiate seat planning earlier.
Employees will not see any seating or group information until team formation is officially closed and results are published.
 
11. Admin Seat Allocation Responsibilities
11.1 Admin Visibility
Admin can view:
All teams and their members
All individual employees not in any team
Seating group capacities
Seating assigned and unassigned employees
11.2 Intelligent Seat Allocation
Admin can allocate seats based on:
Team size
Seating group capacity
Best use of available seats
Ensuring each team sits together in one group
11.3 Manual Intervention
Admin must be able to: 
Manually assign teams or individuals to specific seating groups
Adjust allocations if conflicts arise
Manual intervention is necessary for: 
Large teams
Last-minute constraints
Special seating decisions
11.4 Allocation Rules
No team is split across seating groups.
Seating capacity must never be exceeded.
Individual employees are placed where seats are available.
If a team cannot fit anywhere: 
Admin handles it manually.
 
12. Employee Seat Visibility
12.1 Before Seat Publication
Employees can only see:
Their team members
Invitation status
12.2 After Seat Publication
Employees can see:
Their assigned seating group (A–H)
Members sitting with them
 
13. Export & Reporting
Admin can export final seating arrangements to Excel.
Exported data should include: 
SAP ID
Employee Name
Team identifier
Seating Group
 
14. Audit & Traceability
Platform should maintain history of: 
Invitations sent and accepted
Team formations
Seat allocations
This ensures transparency and dispute resolution.
 
15. Success Criteria
The platform is successful if:
Employees form teams smoothly without confusion.
Individual employees are handled properly.
Seating is allocated without splitting teams.
Seating capacity is fully respected.
Admin can manage, adjust, and export seating easily.
 
16. Assumptions
Seating capacities are finalized before allocation.
Team formation duration is clearly communicated.
Admin decisions are final for seating.
